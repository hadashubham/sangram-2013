Circuit Mania

Introduction :
Circuit Mania is a two level event with circuits as the main flavor and designing as bliss to it. It requires the participants to design various circuits for given problems. However, the levels of these problems are so kept so that students from any year with any diverse engineering background may try their hand in this event.
Description :
Each participant team would be given components (Viz IC's, Resistors, Diodes, Wires, Transistors, etc) for implementing the circuit problem given to them. The basic idea and the concept required shall also be given along with the problem. In order to make students competent enough to participate in this event an optional briefing would be delivered so as to make them well acquainted with the approach of circuit designing. The briefing would include basic concepts required to design the circuits while explaining how to use components that are essentially required in circuit designing such as working on breadboards and few essential IC's. Time duration for the designing of the circuit at each level would be fixed. Team qualifying the second Round (time duration matter) would be considered as the winner of the event.
Rounds :

  • First round will be a technical test (MCQ Type) containing 25 Questions.
  • In second round you have to design a circuit according to the given problem statement.

    • Rules :
  • 1. Each team can have maximum of 2 participants.
  • 2. The duration of each level is around 20 minutes. The duration is fixed and any request for extra time would not be entertained.
  • 3. The components required for the designing would be provided to the team. However, the use of any component beyond what is provided would be prohibited.
  • 4. Circuital Problems along with the necessary basic idea would be given at the instant of starting of the event.
  • 5. Each team would have to get the circuit checked before switching on the main supply.

    • Entry Fee : Rs/- 20
    Prize Money :
  • Prize money for the team Securing 1st rank is 1500 Rs.
  • Prize money for the team Securing 2nd rank is 1000 Rs.

    Introduction :  Race your line tracking robots on arenas with hills and valleys! Speed matters for sure!
    Problem Statement :
    The participants should build an autonomous robot that can follow the given path in minimum possible time, without deviating from the line. The aim of the event is to check the stability and sensing capabilities of the robot in comparison with other opponents.
    Rules :

  • Robot must be fully autonomous. Any link to off-board computers is strictly prohibited.
  • The bot must fit into a cube of 25 cm x25 cm x 25 cm at all times. It may not expand at any point during its run beyond these dimensions.
  • The power supply for the bot should be contained on-board, and should not exceed 24V between any two points in the circuit.
  • Any robot that is deemed by event organizers to be harmful or damaging to either people or the track will be disqualified immediately.
  • A robot can utilize on-board memory to its advantage.

    • Task Specification :
  • The length and layout of the track will be revealed on the day of the event.
  • Other specifications of the track are as follows:-
  • The width of the line will be constant (2cm) throughout the track with a possible error of 5%.
  • The track might also contain few crossovers (places where the line crosses itself), but they will be at 90 degrees to each other.
  • At certain parts , the track may fork into 2 paths.
  • At the beginning of each crossover or fork, there will be a tag provided on any side of the track. It would be advantageous for the bot to proceed along the path indicated by the tag.
  • There could be minor discontinuities (<1cm) in the track.
  • The track will be no closer than 15 cm to the edges of the rectangular arena on which the track is made.
  • The track will be of white color on a black background. However, the white color may not be completely uniform and there might be slight variation in color.
  • The arena is made of wood such that there is sufficient friction for the robot.
  • White tags will be painted on turns. The width of the tags is 2cm and they will be placed at the start of the curvatures. The tags will be 2cm away from the line.
  • No tags shall be provided on simple curvatures.
  • There might be elevations at certain parts of the track of maximum 10 degrees.

    Full Throttle

    Introduction :
    From the moment "GO" begins the race and all throughout you keep dreaming of just one place, The Check line. Gripping the ground and raising the gear you keep moving but finally comes the fear and apart from winning you just don’t care. When your IC engine car hits the race let the track itself say, oh!! Here comes the one that shall show you the way.
    Tasks :
    Make a wireless remote controlled machine, powered by only an IC engine, which can race against other opponents on an off-road dirt track with many obstacles.
    Task
    Make a wireless remote controlled machine, powered by only an IC engine, which can race against other opponents on an off-road dirt track with many obstacles.
    Machine Specification :

  • Machine should fit in a box of dimensions 800mm x 600mm x600mm(depends on the feasibility and size of cooling system) (l x b x h) at any moment of time during the race. The external device which is used to control the machine is not included in the size constraint.
  • The machine should be controlled by a wireless remote control mechanism throughout the race.
  • The machine must not be made from Lego parts, or any ready-made assembly kits other than the parts mentioned below. Readily available chassis layouts are not allowed. Any machine found having a ready-made chassis will be disqualified.
  • The machine may be roughly classified into structural and functional parts:
    Functional parts - Gears, differential gear, engine, springs, shock absorbers, servo motors (non-propulsion purposes only), batteries, wheels, wheel hub are allowed to be used as available in the market. Structural parts - Chassis, steering mechanism, shock towers, suspension (excluding upper suspension arm, suspension spring and shock absorbers), have to be built by the participants themselves. Judging for the same will be strict and the participant will be immediately disqualified if any of the above structural components are found to be ready-made.
  • The tires used must have a minimum diameter of 3 inches. You are advised to use tires of good width for better performance on dirt tracks.
  • Brake Mechanism: It is compulsory to incorporate braking mechanism in the car. Participants have to fabricate the brake pad as well as brake disk as a part of the braking mechanism. Any other part used in braking mechanism can be ready-made.
  • Wheel Hub: Any part rigidly attached to the wheel hub will be considered as a part of it and hence can be ready-made. An example here is that of the ball stud.
  • Steering Mechanism: Any part which is connected to steering rod rigidly i.e. has no degrees of freedom with respect to steering rod (example: heim joint) will be considered as part of steering rod and thus has to be fabricated by participants.
  • Suspension mechanism: Any part rigidly connected to suspension arms or one with no degrees of freedom with respect to suspension arm will be considered as its part and has to be fabricated by the participants, except the upper suspension arm. For example both the heim joint for the upper suspension arm and the stud rigidly connected to the wheel hub can be bought from the market.
  • If there are parts used in the concerned joint which are neither rigidly connected with suspension or the hub, steering system or hub; they can be used ready-made from the market.
  • The above pictures are just sample pictures for you to understand the rules easily.

  • The teams may obtain a time slot for practice before first round of the game. The number of practice slots will be limited and the slots will be given on a first come first served basis. The teams will get to use the track only during the time of their practice slot. Any requests for using the track at any other time will not be entertained.
  • Any team that is not ready at the time specified will be eliminated from the competition automatically.
  • The teams must adhere to the spirit of healthy competition. The teams must not damage the opponent's machine in any way. Judges reserve the right to disqualify any team indulged in misbehavior.
  • The name of your machine should be prominently displayed on the machine.
  • Judges decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.
  • The organizers reserve the rights to change any or all of the above rules as they deem fit. Change in rules, if any will be highlighted on the website and notified to the registered participants.
